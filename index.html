Research Interest

Research

My research interests span three primary areas:

  • Large Language Models (LLMs).
    I study the reasoning and adaptation capabilities of LLMs, including graph-enhanced reasoning models such as GraphLLM and the use of GNNs as effective structural adapters for LLMs(Graph-Adapterem>). I also examine model robustness and abstraction abilities through tasks like the Concept-Reversed Winograd Schema Challenge (CR-WSC>).

  • Agent Systems.
    My work explores the RAG systems in conversational agents, controllable decision-making, and mechanisms that enable multi-step planning and tool-use.

  • AI for Science (Biology & Healthcare).
    I develop machine learning methods for dynamic scientific and biomedical systems, including neural ODE–based approaches for brain signal analysis (BrainODE), models for longitudinal disease progression forecasting, generative modeling for physiological time-series such as ECG and EGG, and data-driven frameworks for simulating complex physical or biological processes.
For a complete list of my publications, please refer to my Google Scholar.
